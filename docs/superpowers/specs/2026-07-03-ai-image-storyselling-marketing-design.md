# AI Image Storyselling Marketing Design

## Project Context

Project: Bia Thay Tu public B2C website and social content system.

Brand position: premium imported German beer and German wine, with core cues around heritage, German brewing discipline, official imports, hosting, gifting, and informed enjoyment.

Primary goals for this image plan:

- Build a premium visual identity for Bia Thay Tu.
- Produce repeatable image content for Facebook, SEO articles, and satellite social pages.
- Use storyselling, not isolated product glamour shots.

Approved creative direction: **Nguon Goc -> Chiec Ly**.

The image system should tell a small story: German origin, brewing heritage, careful serving, then the concrete moment of enjoying the beer at home, at a hosting table, or as a considered gift.

## Strategic Choice

Three execution approaches were considered:

1. **Pure AI lifestyle generation**
   - Fastest and cheapest for high volume.
   - Weakness: AI can distort product labels, packaging, glass shape, alcohol cues, and brand details.
   - Use only for background mood boards or generic scene exploration.

2. **Hybrid AI background + official product compositing**
   - Generate premium backgrounds, table settings, monastery-inspired scenes, macro pours, and food-pairing environments with AI.
   - Composite official product assets from `public/images/products/official/` into the final image.
   - Best balance of speed, brand safety, and product accuracy.
   - Recommended.

3. **Full editorial design system**
   - Build reusable templates for carousels, SEO covers, comparison visuals, and infographic posts.
   - Strong for scale and consistency, but slower to start.
   - Best as phase two after the hybrid image language is validated.

Recommended path: start with approach 2, then turn winning layouts into approach 3 templates.

## Visual Language

Core look:

- Deep navy, dark wood, warm gold, amber beer, soft cream foam.
- Cinematic side light, restrained contrast, realistic shadows.
- Bavaria / monastery cues without making the scene look like fantasy art.
- Vietnamese hosting context where useful: dining table, seafood, grilled food, balcony, home dinner, partner meeting.
- Product remains sharp and accurate; background can be atmospheric.

Avoid:

- AI-generated labels or unreadable fake text on cans/bottles.
- Overly medieval, religious, or fantasy monastery visuals.
- Cheap party visuals, chaotic beer tables, club/nightlife mood.
- Underage-looking people, reckless drinking, driving scenes with alcohol.
- Health claims for beer. For Bitburger 0.0%, position as alcohol-free lifestyle, not medical or performance benefit.

## Storyselling Formula

Every image concept should follow this sequence:

1. **Origin hook:** a detail from Germany, monastery heritage, Reinheitsgebot, Bavaria, Eifel, or official import.
2. **Sensory bridge:** foam, pour, amber color, malt, hops, glassware, serving temperature, food pairing.
3. **Vietnam moment:** home table, hosting, gifting, weekend dinner, learning to drink better.
4. **Product reason:** which product fits this moment and why.
5. **CTA:** save, inbox, order set, ask for pairing, or read the full article.

Example copy spine:

> Tu vien 400 nam khong nam trong chiec nhan. No nam trong cach ban rot chai bia toi nay: dung ly, dung nhiet do, dung khoanh khac.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Heritage To Glass

Purpose: premium brand building.

Use for:

- Homepage/website hero refreshes.
- Facebook cover/key visual.
- SEO article covers about Benediktiner, Bitburger, Reinheitsgebot, German beer history.

Image ideas:

- Brown Benediktiner bottle and Weizen glass in warm gold light, with subtle Bavarian monastery arch in the background.
- Bitburger Pils glass in front of a refined Eifel-inspired dark green and gold backdrop.
- Official import visual: product on table with document-like texture, stamp motif, and restrained gold accent.

### 2. Ritual Of Pouring

Purpose: educational content that builds authority and gets saves.

Use for:

- "Cach rot bia lua mi" posts.
- Serving temperature content.
- Glassware education.
- Macro shots for reels covers and carousels.

Image ideas:

- Foam rising in a tall Weizen glass, product bottle composited beside it.
- Three-step pour sequence: tilt, straighten, foam cap.
- Close-up condensation and foam texture with minimal text overlay area.

### 3. Host Knows What To Serve

Purpose: soft conversion through real-life use cases.

Use for:

- Tiep khach / gift / dinner table posts.
- Product set suggestions.
- B2B gifting and partner-hosting visuals.

Image ideas:

- Premium home table for 4-6 adults, one hero bottle/glass, cold cuts or grilled food.
- Gift set composition with Benediktiner bottles, glassware, opener, and deep navy wrapping.
- "Khach hoi: bia gi day?" moment, shown as a quiet table detail rather than faces.

### 4. Germany Meets Vietnamese Food

Purpose: steady social content with high relatability.

Use for:

- Food pairing posts.
- Satellite page "Bia & Am Thuc".
- SEO covers for pairing articles.

Image ideas:

- Weissbier with seafood or grilled chicken.
- Dunkel with BBQ ribs, beef stew, or roasted meat.
- Pilsner with pizza, fried snacks, or light Vietnamese dishes.

## Production Workflow

1. Select product and story angle.
2. Pick output format: feed, carousel, story/reel cover, SEO cover, or ad creative.
3. Generate AI background or scene with no readable label text.
4. Composite official product image from the product asset library.
5. Add restrained typography only after image generation, in Canva, Figma, Photoshop, or code.
6. Export aspect ratios:
   - Feed: 4:5
   - Square fallback: 1:1
   - SEO/blog: 16:9
   - Story/reel cover: 9:16
7. Run QA checklist before publishing.

## Prompt Framework

Use this structure:

```text
Premium cinematic product background for imported German beer marketing.
Scene: [heritage / pouring ritual / hosting table / food pairing].
Mood: deep navy, warm gold, dark wood, refined, realistic, quiet luxury.
Composition: leave clean space for official product compositing on [left/right/center].
Lighting: warm side light, natural reflections, realistic shadows.
Camera: 85mm editorial product photography, shallow depth of field.
Details: amber beer, cream foam, clean glassware, subtle Bavarian/German cues.
Constraints: no readable text, no fake brand labels, no people under 30, no drunk behavior, no driving, no fantasy style.
```

```text
Premium cinematic background for imported German wheat beer marketing, inspired by a Bavarian monastery cellar but realistic and restrained. ```

```

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, each image must pass:

- Product label and packaging are official or composited from official assets.
- No AI-created fake label text appears.
- The image does not imply unsafe drinking, driving after drinking, or underage consumption.
- If people appear, they look clearly adult and are not intoxicated.
- Serving glass, foam, color, and food pairing are plausible for the product.
- Visual style matches deep navy / gold premium identity.
- There is enough negative space for caption or post overlay when needed.
- CTA and copy do not overclaim health benefits.
- 18+ / responsible drinking note is added where required for alcohol content.

```

```

## Success Metrics

Track separately by content pillar:

- Saves and shares for education/ritual posts.
- Comments and inbox messages for hosting/food-pairing posts.
- Product page clicks and cart actions for conversion images.
- Organic search CTR for SEO cover images.
- Repeatable prompt/template winners after 30 days.

The first month is successful if at least two visual formulas are strong enough to become repeatable templates: one for heritage brand building and one for practical hosting/conversion.
